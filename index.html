Marketing Domain · Know → Reach → Convert → Retain

Complete Marketing Coverage

23 modules across 4 layers. Full theoretical coverage: STP, Consumer Behaviour, IMC, Analytics, Product Marketing.

Know — understand market, customers, and brand before acting

Intelligence layer. Feeds every other layer. No campaign runs well without Know informing who to target, what they want, and how the brand is perceived right now.

KOL + Influencer Analyser
intelligence
Reach quality, engagement, CTR, ROI, turn rate. Audience fit score vs campaign target — warn below 50%, block below 25%.
Sentiment + Social Listening
intelligence
Brand + competitor sentiment tracking. 25pt/4hr threshold → campaign pause alert. Crisis SOP auto-activates in Chatbot.
Marketing Analytics
analytics
Channel ROI, funnel attribution, 48hr proactive alert. Context-tagged results. Predictive forecasting absorbed as forward-view toggle.
Dynamic CRM + Personalisation
intelligence
Behavioural segments + triggers. 30-min sync — converts removed immediately. Message sequencing logic absorbed — defines narrative arc per relationship stage.
Competitor Monitor
intelligencenew v6
3–5 named competitors tracked continuously — price changes, launches, social activity, review sentiment. Upgrades Stage 1 one-time snapshot into live feed.
Customer Journey Mapper
intelligencenew v6
Full path: first touch → purchase → repeat. Connects Lead Capture → CRM → Attribution → Retention. Shows where customers drop off and where they convert.
Behavioural Insights Engine
intelligencenew v6
Covers the "why" — zero-party data and motivation intelligence. Weekly domain trend digest (auto-pushed). Passive micro-surveys at trigger moments. Owner reads results, never designs research.
Design: Hybrid. Weekly digest automatic. Surveys passive. Active custom question available but never required.
◉ Brand Health — Performance Dashboard gauge
Composite score from Sentiment, Reviews, Customer Journey, and Post-Purchase Feedback. Updated weekly. One number, one interpretation line. "Brand perception aligned" or "Positioning drift detected." Drill-down available. No configuration required by owner.

Reach — get in front of the right people through every relevant channel

Acquisition layer. SEO, GEO, paid social with CRM-powered targeting, direct messaging, campaign orchestration, and research-driven audience building. Every modern acquisition channel covered.

Search Presence
acquisitionnew v6
Two engines, one surface. SEO: keyword tracking, on-page optimisation, organic ranking. GEO: LLM visibility audit, AI-optimised content, citation building so AI assistants recommend the brand.
GEO note: Positioned as "make AI recommend your brand" — measurement is probabilistic, not precise. Honest and correct.
Smart Ads Engine
acquisitionnew v6
Meta + TikTok ad deployment + CRM-powered audiences. Lookalike (top-LTV customers → find similar people), exclusion (remove existing pipeline), re-engagement (cold leads → targeted re-activation).
Selling point: Targets from real customer data inside SLC — typically 3–5× higher conversion than cold demographic targeting.
Email + LINE Broadcast
acquisitionnew v6
Mass direct communication — separate from 1-to-1 Chatbot. Email newsletters, LINE OA broadcast, segmented sequences. Message sequencing absorbed: defines narrative arc per contact relationship stage.
Campaign Builder
acquisition
Full campaign from one brief. Audience overlap warning, KOL scheduling confirmation. IMC Consistency Check absorbed: pre-launch cross-channel coherence validation before any campaign goes live.
AI Lead Capture
acquisition
In-chat micro-flow + web form. AI qualifies intent, source-tags by campaign code, pushes to CRM with score. Non-converters captured before chat closes.
Market Pulse
researchnew v6
Primary research without the burden. Weekly category trend digest. Passive triggered surveys. "What should I launch next?" answered by customer responses. Owner reads results — never designs the study.

Convert — turn attention into sales efficiently and consistently

Execution layer. Content production, discount management, attribution tracking, channel scheduling, and the customer-facing conversation interface. Where marketing intelligence becomes revenue.

AI Content Generator
execution
Captions, ad copy, email drafts, image prompts from business profile. Variants mode: 2–3 creatives per campaign for A/B testing. Content Strategy (Stage 1) plans — this executes.
Promotion Rules Engine
execution
Owner defines rules. Chatbot applies autonomously. One-code-per-order global default. Stacking requires explicit permission. Applied discounts logged in Auditor.
Campaign Attribution
execution
Unique codes per KOL/campaign. Source-tagged orders. Last-touch default. Multi-campaign orders handled cleanly. Results stored with context alongside revenue figures.
Marketing Ops Agent
execution
Scheduling, ad monitoring, A/B test rotation (shifts budget to winner at 48hr), monthly reports. Receives from Content Generator — publishes, tracks, feeds back to Analytics.
Business Chatbot + Centralised Inbox
execution
Line OA, IG DM, FB Messenger, WhatsApp, email — unified. E-slip verifier. Live inventory query. Promo Rules Engine. In-chat lead capture. Crisis SOP mode on sentiment threshold.

Operations Domain · Orders → Money → Operations → People+Process → Strategy

Complete Operations Coverage

21 modules across 5 layers. COO lens at SME scale — every module answers a question a solopreneur asks daily. Complexity stays in the system; owner navigates by intent.

Orders — the order-to-cash cycle, fully closed

Every order from confirmation to delivered and closed. One central record. Without this, each order exists as fragmented events across Chatbot, Auditor, and Courier — manageable at 5 orders/day, breaks at 50. Order Management Hub is the backbone the whole Ops Engine connects to.

Order Management Hub
ordersnew v7
Central order record — one status per order from confirmed to delivered and closed. Connects: Chatbot (order created), Inventory (stock reserved), Auditor (revenue logged), Courier (shipment triggered), Returns (exception handling). The operational backbone of Stage 2.
COO note: Without this, every module is an island. At scale, "where is order #247?" has no answer in the current architecture. This fixes it.
Quote + Invoice Manager
orders
Quotes, invoices, payment tracking, overdue chasing. Revenue data feeds Basic Auditor. Connects to Order Hub as the billing record layer.
Returns + Refund Manager
orders
Return → chatbot logs → immediate human flag → owner resolves → agent records. Refund logged in Auditor. Stock disposition: restock/write-off/quarantine. Human-in-the-loop by design.
Courier Integration
orders
Kerry, Flash, J&T APIs. Auto-book on paid + packed. Label generation. Tracking pushed to customer via original channel. Delivery status feeds Order Hub and Performance Dashboard.

Money — full financial visibility, forward and backward

Historical (what happened), operational (what's owed), and forward (what's coming). Three different financial questions, three modules. The COO gap in v6 was that SLC knew what came in but was blind on what goes out and what's coming next.

Basic Auditor
finance
Historical view. P&L, cash flow, actuals vs Stage 1 projections overlay. Campaign results stored with context. Receives confirmed inflows from Order Hub and outflows from Accounts Payable Tracker.
Rolling Cash Flow Forecast
financenew v7
Forward view. 30/60/90 day projection at the transaction level. Pulls confirmed orders (revenue expected), accounts payable (outflows scheduled), recurring costs from Ops Scheduler, and restock commitments from PO Generator. Answers: "Will I have cash on the 15th to pay my supplier?"
COO note: Most important unanswered financial question at SME scale. Auditor shows the past — this shows the future. Standalone because it needs its own screen and its own data model.
Accounts Payable Tracker
financenew v7
Tracks every outgoing payment obligation — supplier invoices, freelancer fees, courier charges, platform commissions. Matches against POs from PO Generator. Alerts on due dates. Feeds Auditor as confirmed outflows and Rolling Cash Flow Forecast as scheduled future outflows.
COO note: Auditor only records money coming in unless manually told about outflows. AP Tracker is the other half of the financial picture — what is owed and when.
Performance Dashboard
finance
Real-time single view — all Stage 2 modules. Revenue (invoiced/collected/outstanding), pipeline, cash position, open tasks, low stock, churn risk, Brand Health gauge, OKR progress. First screen on login.

Operations — managing stock, quality, suppliers, and capacity

Five modules covering the physical and supplier layer of operations. The new additions — Batch + Quality Log and Capacity Planner — cover the two gaps a COO would flag immediately: "can we trace a bad batch?" and "do we have the capacity to fulfil what we've sold?"

Inventory Keeper
ops
Live stock levels per SKU. Chatbot queries via API before every availability reply — not static FAQ. Low-stock triggers PO Generator. Feeds Order Hub for stock reservation at order confirmation.
Purchase Order Generator
ops
Auto-drafts PO from Vendor Manager data when Inventory hits threshold. Sends to supplier via centralised inbox (Line/WhatsApp). Tracks: sent → confirmed → ETA → received → stocked. On received: Inventory auto-updates.
Batch + Quality Log
opsnew v7
Product traceability for product-based businesses. Each batch: date made, ingredients/materials used, quantity produced, quality check result (pass/fail/hold), and which customer orders were fulfilled from this batch. If a complaint arrives, trace instantly which customers are affected.
Diagnostic activation: Product-based businesses only. Not relevant for service businesses. At SME scale this doesn't need to be ISO-level — it needs to answer "which batch was that and who got it?"
Vendor + People Manager
ops
Freelancers, part-timers, suppliers — rates, contacts, performance notes, contract status. Source data for PO Generator and Accounts Payable Tracker.
Capacity Planner
opsnew v7
Given active orders (from Order Hub) + scheduled tasks (from Ops Scheduler) + available people (from Vendor Manager) — does the business have enough bandwidth this week? One screen. Traffic light: green (capacity available), amber (near limit), red (overloaded). Alerts owner before commitments exceed capacity.
COO note: Not resource planning software. Just an overload warning system. The question every SME owner asks Monday morning — answered by the system, not by mental maths.

People + Process — keeping operations consistent and compliant

Four modules covering task execution, documented procedures, legal compliance deadlines, and communication capture. The compliance calendar is the addition most likely to prevent a crisis — a lapsed licence or missed tax filing is the kind of operational failure that has nothing to do with the business and everything to do with the founder being too busy to track a deadline.

Ops Scheduler
process
Task management + recurring workflows. Receives action items from Call Summary. Tasks link directly to SOP Library entries. Open task count feeds Performance Dashboard.
SOP + Knowledge Base
process
Chatbot's dynamic FAQ source. Crisis SOP template — activates on sentiment alert. How-to docs, runbooks, onboarding procedures. Strongest retention mechanic — once stored, switching cost is very high.
Compliance Calendar
processnew v7
Tracks regulatory deadlines — business licence renewal, VAT filing, social security contributions, food safety permits, annual accounts filing. Alerts at 30 days, 7 days, and day-of. Creates tasks in Ops Scheduler automatically. Thai-specific compliance requirements pre-loaded by business type from diagnostic profile.
COO note: A lapsed food safety permit or missed tax filing is not a business strategy failure — it's an operational oversight. This prevents it. High impact, low build cost.
AI Call + Meeting Summary
comms
Upload or record call. AI transcribes, extracts key points and action items. Dual push: summary → CRM contact record AND action items → Ops Scheduler tasks simultaneously.

Strategy — setting targets, adapting the model, planning the exit

Three modules at the strategic layer. OKR Tracker is the addition — it's the difference between watching metrics and managing toward outcomes. A solopreneur who sets a ฿50,000 revenue target for the month and can see daily progress is running a business. One who just watches the dashboard is hoping.

OKR / Goal Tracker
strategynew v7
Owner sets monthly targets: revenue, new customers, on-time delivery rate, average response time, repeat purchase rate. Dashboard shows progress as percentage toward each goal. Performance Dashboard shows headline score. Simple and visual — not enterprise OKR software.
COO note: The difference between a speedometer and a sat-nav. Metrics show speed. Goals show whether you're heading in the right direction. SLC had metrics. Now it has direction.
Pivot Suggester
strategy
Model changes with recalculated financials — channel pivot, target pivot, product pivot, model pivot, scale pivot. Stage 2 only — requires real performance data from Auditor, CRM, and Inventory.
Exit Strategy Planner
strategy
Sell, shutdown, downsize, full pivot, partner/investor — valuations and downside math. Knowing the exit makes the entry feel safer. Counterintuitively increases operating confidence.
