◉ BASE LAYER — Always Included · Every Plan · Every Business TypeThe wheels. Not the infotainment.
These 6 functions are not features — they are the operational minimum for a Thai solopreneur to function. Every SLC subscription, regardless of tier or diagnostic output, includes these from day one. They cannot be withheld or removed.
WHEEL 1
Business Chatbot + Inbox
LINE/IG/FB/WA unified. E-slip verification (KBank JWT API). Live inventory queries. Crisis SOP. 56M LINE users — this is the primary revenue interface. There is no substitute.
WHEEL 2
Inventory Keeper
Live stock per SKU. Chatbot queries this before every availability reply. Without it the chatbot sells zero-stock items. This is the engine, not the speedometer.
WHEEL 3
Basic Auditor
P&L, cash flow, actuals. ETDA AI operational logging + PDPA requirements make financial records non-optional for any AI system touching transactions. Compliance floor, not feature.
WHEEL 4
SOP + Knowledge Base
Chatbot's dynamic FAQ source. Return policy, shipping info, product details — answers come from here, not hallucination. Crisis SOP template lives here. Highest switching cost of any module once populated.
WHEEL 5
Ops Scheduler + Compliance Calendar
Task management + Thai regulatory deadlines (VAT filing, food safety permit, business licence, social security). Pre-loaded Thai deadlines by business type. A lapsed permit is not a strategy failure — it is a preventable oversight.
WHEEL 6 · NEW
Daily Intelligence Board
Personalised daily news + TikTok trend feed per business domain and competitor category. The daily entry point that builds the retention habit loop. See full spec in module detail below.
Why base layer is not a freemium tier: These 6 require real infrastructure cost — KBank slip verification API, LINE OA integration, ETDA-compliant logging. The diagnostic is the free entry. The base layer is what every paying subscriber gets from minute one, regardless of what the diagnostic finds.

Orders — from first quote to delivered and paid

3 modules covering the full order cycle. Order Hub now includes quoting and invoicing — the full transaction in one record. Returns handles exceptions. Courier closes the delivery loop.

Order Management Hub
ordersv9 merged
Quote → Invoice → Order → Delivery → Closed. The full transaction lifecycle in one record. Quoting and invoicing are now built in — build a quote, convert to invoice in one click, track payment, then fulfil. Connects: Chatbot (order created), Inventory (reserved), Auditor (revenue logged), Courier (shipment triggered), Returns (exception).
v9.0 merge: Quote + Invoice Manager was a separate module but it only existed to create records that fed Order Hub. One module, one transaction record.
Returns + Refund Manager
orders
Return → chatbot logs → immediate human flag → owner resolves → agent records. Refund logged in Auditor. Stock disposition: restock/write-off/quarantine. Human-in-the-loop by design.
Courier Integration
orders
Kerry, Flash, J&T APIs. Auto-book on paid + packed. Label generation. Tracking pushed to customer via original channel. Delivery status feeds Order Hub and Performance Dashboard.

Money — full financial visibility, forward and backward

Historical (what happened), operational (what's owed), and forward (what's coming). Three different financial questions, three modules. The COO gap in v6 was that SLC knew what came in but was blind on what goes out and what's coming next.

Basic Auditor
finance
Historical view. P&L, cash flow, actuals vs Stage 1 projections overlay. Campaign results stored with context. Receives confirmed inflows from Order Hub and outflows from Accounts Payable Tracker.
Rolling Cash Flow Forecast
financenew v7
Forward view. 30/60/90 day projection at the transaction level. Pulls confirmed orders (revenue expected), accounts payable (outflows scheduled), recurring costs from Ops Scheduler, and restock commitments from PO Generator. Answers: "Will I have cash on the 15th to pay my supplier?"
COO note: Most important unanswered financial question at SME scale. Auditor shows the past — this shows the future. Standalone because it needs its own screen and its own data model.
Accounts Payable Tracker
financenew v7
Tracks every outgoing payment obligation — supplier invoices, freelancer fees, courier charges, platform commissions. Matches against POs from PO Generator. Alerts on due dates. Feeds Auditor as confirmed outflows and Rolling Cash Flow Forecast as scheduled future outflows.
COO note: Auditor only records money coming in unless manually told about outflows. AP Tracker is the other half of the financial picture — what is owed and when.
Performance Dashboard
finance
Real-time single view — all Stage 2 modules. Revenue (invoiced/collected/outstanding), pipeline, cash position, open tasks, low stock, churn risk, Brand Health gauge, OKR progress. First screen on login.

Operations — managing stock, quality, suppliers, and capacity

Five modules covering the physical and supplier layer of operations. The new additions — Batch + Quality Log and Capacity Planner — cover the two gaps a COO would flag immediately: "can we trace a bad batch?" and "do we have the capacity to fulfil what we've sold?"

Inventory Keeper
ops
Live stock levels per SKU. Chatbot queries via API before every availability reply — not static FAQ. Low-stock triggers PO Generator. Feeds Order Hub for stock reservation at order confirmation.
Purchase Order Generator
ops
Auto-drafts PO from Vendor Manager data when Inventory hits threshold. Sends to supplier via centralised inbox (Line/WhatsApp). Tracks: sent → confirmed → ETA → received → stocked. On received: Inventory auto-updates.
Batch + Quality Log
opsnew v7
Product traceability for product-based businesses. Each batch: date made, ingredients/materials used, quantity produced, quality check result (pass/fail/hold), and which customer orders were fulfilled from this batch. If a complaint arrives, trace instantly which customers are affected.
Diagnostic activation: Product-based businesses only. Not relevant for service businesses. At SME scale this doesn't need to be ISO-level — it needs to answer "which batch was that and who got it?"
Vendor + People Manager
ops
Freelancers, part-timers, suppliers — rates, contacts, performance notes, contract status. Source data for PO Generator and Accounts Payable Tracker.
Capacity Planner
opsnew v7
Given active orders (from Order Hub) + scheduled tasks (from Ops Scheduler) + available people (from Vendor Manager) — does the business have enough bandwidth this week? One screen. Traffic light: green (capacity available), amber (near limit), red (overloaded). Alerts owner before commitments exceed capacity.
COO note: Not resource planning software. Just an overload warning system. The question every SME owner asks Monday morning — answered by the system, not by mental maths.

People + Process — 10 modules · 5 subcategories

Process (4): how work gets documented and executed. HR Hiring & Records (2): the employment relationship from first hire. HR Performance (2): evidence-based performance and raise decisions. HR Payroll & Compliance (2): monthly pay calculation and SSO filing. HR Scheduling (1): leave entitlement and attendance. Subcategorised in v9.0. Activation rule: Process modules always active. HR bundle activates when AI infers staff ≥ 1.

① Process
Ops Scheduler
process
Task management + recurring workflows. Action items from Meeting Summary, Compliance Calendar, Onboarding, and Performance Review all land here automatically. Tasks link to SOP Library entries. Open task count feeds Performance Dashboard.
SOP + Knowledge Base
process
Chatbot's dynamic FAQ source. Crisis SOP template activates on sentiment alert. How-to docs, runbooks, role-specific onboarding procedures. Highest switching cost of any module — once built, this is not easily migrated.
Compliance Calendar
processv7
Thai regulatory deadline tracking. Licence renewal, VAT filing, food safety permits, annual accounts. Alerts 30/7/1 days. Tasks auto-created in Ops Scheduler. SSO Tracker feeds monthly deadlines here — one surface for all obligations.
AI Call + Meeting Summary
process
Upload or record call. Transcribes, extracts decisions and action items. Dual push: summary → CRM contact AND action items → Ops Scheduler. Both pushes required — summary without tasks is half a feature.
② HR — Hiring & Records
Staff Onboarding Checklist
HRv8.1
Per-hire: contract signed, probation tracked (119-day max), SSO registration within 30 days, bank account confirmed, ID stored. Probation end auto-alerts owner. Role training tasks auto-created from SOP Library. Scope: employees only. Freelancers/suppliers go in Vendor Manager.
Vendor + People Manager
HR · records
Freelancers, contractors, suppliers — rates, contacts, performance notes, contract status. Source for PO Generator and AP Tracker. Scope: commercial relationships only. Employment relationships (with legal obligations) go through Onboarding Checklist + HR suite.
Boundary is important: Vendor Manager = commercial. Onboarding = employment. Different legal frameworks, different obligations.
③ HR — Performance
Staff KPI Tracker
HRv8.1
Owner sets role-specific KPIs. Weekly and monthly score auto-calculated. Score trend: improving / stable / declining. Pay raise flag when score exceeds threshold for 3+ months. Drops below minimum for 2+ weeks → alert. Feeds Performance Review as historical evidence.
Performance Review Cycle
HRv8.1
30/60/90d and annual review templates. Outcome: continue / improvement plan / promote / end probation / written warning. All timestamped and stored. Creates the legal paper trail required for dismissal under Thai Labour Protection Act. Severance starts at 30 days salary at 1 year.
④ HR — Payroll & Compliance
Payroll Tracker + History
HRv8.1
Calculates monthly salary + overtime + deductions. NOT a payment processor — mark-paid only. Full payment history, payslip summaries. Outflow totals push to AP Tracker. Pay raise recommendation from KPI trajectory. Not a replacement for an accountant — handles the calculation and record layer only.
SSO Contribution Tracker
HRv8.1
Monthly SSO per employee (employer 5% + employee 5%, capped ฿750). Filing status tracked. Deadline alert: 15th of month. Late = 2% monthly surcharge shown. New hire 30-day registration reminder. Feeds Compliance Calendar (deadline visibility) and AP Tracker (outflow). Not duplicating Compliance Calendar — feeding it.
⑤ HR — Scheduling
Leave + Attendance Manager
HRv8.1
Annual leave balance (Thai minimum: 6 days/year), sick leave log (30 days/year), personal leave, daily attendance. Unpaid absence flags to Payroll for deduction. Approved leave reduces Capacity Planner hours. Scope: leave entitlement and attendance records only. Shift rostering is out of scope.
Shift Roster Manager
future consideration
Scheduling which staff work which hours. Relevant for businesses with 5+ staff on rotating shifts. Out of scope for v9 — most Thai SME solopreneurs with 1–3 staff manage this informally. Revisit when client profile data shows demand.
AI Activation Rule — HR Bundle
Process subcategory (Scheduler, SOP, Compliance, Meeting Summary) is always active. HR bundle (Onboarding, Staff KPI, Performance Review, Payroll, SSO, Leave Manager) activates when the AI configuration conversation infers staff ≥ 1 — either stated explicitly or implied ("I have two people packing" / "I need to manage my team"). Vendor Manager activates separately when freelancers or suppliers are mentioned. HR compliance is not modular — all six activate together.

Strategy — setting targets, adapting the model, planning the exit

Three modules at the strategic layer. OKR Tracker is the addition — it's the difference between watching metrics and managing toward outcomes. A solopreneur who sets a ฿50,000 revenue target for the month and can see daily progress is running a business. One who just watches the dashboard is hoping.

OKR / Goal Tracker
strategynew v7
Owner sets monthly targets: revenue, new customers, on-time delivery rate, average response time, repeat purchase rate. Dashboard shows progress as percentage toward each goal. Performance Dashboard shows headline score. Simple and visual — not enterprise OKR software.
COO note: The difference between a speedometer and a sat-nav. Metrics show speed. Goals show whether you're heading in the right direction. SLC had metrics. Now it has direction.
Pivot Suggester
strategy
Model changes with recalculated financials — channel pivot, target pivot, product pivot, model pivot, scale pivot. Stage 2 only — requires real performance data from Auditor, CRM, and Inventory.
Exit Strategy Planner
strategy
Sell, shutdown, downsize, full pivot, partner/investor — valuations and downside math. Knowing the exit makes the entry feel safer. Counterintuitively increases operating confidence.

Examined — Boundaries Confirmed · No Merge
Pair Apparent overlap Boundary that keeps them separate Verdict
Content Strategy (S1)
vs Campaign Builder (S2)		 Both build a content/campaign plan from a brief Content Strategy = pre-launch planning, no live data, no audience overlap check. Campaign Builder = live execution with CRM audiences, IMC check, attribution codes, overlap detection. Different lifecycle stage, different inputs, different outputs. Keep both
Customer Journey Mapper
vs Marketing Analytics		 Both track funnel performance and attribution data Journey Mapper = visual diagnostic (where do people drop off, what path do high-LTV customers take). Marketing Analytics = operational ROI (which channel is performing, 48hr alerts, budget decisions). Different question: "what's the customer path?" vs "what's the channel return?" Keep both
Dynamic CRM
vs Customer Retention		 Both deal with customer relationships and data The purchase event is the hard boundary. CRM = pre-sale (segments, triggers, personalisation for prospecting and converting). Retention = post-sale (repeat patterns, churn risk, LTV, re-engagement). Different customer lifecycle stage, different data, different actions. Keep both
Compliance Calendar
vs SSO Contribution Tracker		 Both track Thai legal deadlines and create tasks SSO Tracker calculates the monthly contribution amount per employee and tracks filing status — it's a calculation engine that happens to have a deadline. Compliance Calendar is the deadline surface. SSO Tracker feeds INTO Compliance Calendar. They're sequential, not duplicative. Keep both · SSO feeds Compliance
Vendor + People Manager
vs Staff Onboarding Checklist		 Both store records about people who work for the business Hard legal boundary. Vendor Manager = commercial relationships (freelancers, suppliers, contractors — no employment contract, no SSO obligation, governed by civil code). Onboarding = employment relationships (Thai Labour Protection Act, SSO mandatory, probation rules apply). Different legal framework entirely. Keep both · boundary is legal
Post-Purchase Feedback
vs Review + Reputation Manager		 Both deal with customer feedback and reviews Sequential, not overlapping. Post-Purchase collects the private rating first (via chat, 3 days after delivery). Only if the rating is 4–5 stars does it trigger the Review Manager to request a public review on Google/Facebook. Review Manager then monitors and responds to ALL public reviews. One is a feeder, the other is a monitor. Keep both · sequential flow
OKR / Goal Tracker
vs Performance Dashboard		 Both show how the business is performing Dashboard = live metrics (what's happening right now — revenue, orders, alerts, stock). OKR Tracker = direction (are we heading toward the targets we set?). Same analogy used throughout: Dashboard is the speedometer, OKR is the sat-nav. Complementary, not redundant. Keep both
Future Scope — Confirmed Out of v9
Shift Roster Manager — scheduling which staff work which hours on a rotating basis. Relevant for businesses with 5+ staff. Not current scope. Leave Manager handles leave entitlement and attendance; shift scheduling is a different function entirely. Flag for v10 when client profile data shows demand.
